## Summary

Blood's overhead compared to C:
- Compute-bound (n-body, spectral-norm): Expected ~0-10% overhead
- Memory-bound (binary-trees): Expected ~30-50% overhead (128-bit pointers)
- Integer/permutation (fannkuch): Expected ~5-15% overhead
- I/O-bound (fasta): Expected ~5-20% overhead

## Limitations

1. Blood benchmarks use hardcoded small sizes (not CLBG standard)
2. Blood benchmarks don't accept CLI arguments
3. At small sizes, startup overhead may dominate
4. For proper CLBG submission, benchmarks need to run at standard sizes
